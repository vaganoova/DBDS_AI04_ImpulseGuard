# Problem Analysis

## Problem Statement

Impulse buying is a common financial behavior that often leads to unnecessary
spending. Current financial systems rarely provide real-time behavioral feedback
before a purchase is completed.

ImpulseGuard aims to use AI to detect impulsive purchases based on user
spending patterns and transaction behavior.

---

## Research Background

Studies show the scale of impulse buying is significant:

- 84–89% of all shoppers have made at least one impulse purchase
- American consumers spend an average of $5,400 per year on unplanned purchases
- Impulse buying accounts for 40–80% of all retail purchases
- 44% of buyers feel regret after an impulse purchase

---

## Why Current Systems Fall Short

Most financial apps and payment systems only track spending *after* it happens.
They show you what you spent last month — but don't warn you *before* you click
"buy now." ImpulseGuard addresses this gap by predicting impulsive behavior at
the moment of purchase.

---

## Key Behavioral Triggers

Research identifies four main factors that drive impulse buying:

| Factor | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Time of day | 74% of impulse purchases happen at night (22:00–05:00) |
| Price | 60.7% of impulse buyers have spent $100+ on a single unplanned purchase |
| Product category | Clothing (55%), beauty, and entertainment are the highest-risk categories |
| Purchase frequency | Repeated buying in the same category indicates impulsive tendencies |

These four factors directly correspond to the features used in our dataset.
